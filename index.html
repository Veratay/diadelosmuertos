Dia De Los Muertos Cemeteries

By Sidney Robinson, Gerry McCarthy, and Thomas Fasan

Hey! In this website you will be able to see how a cemetery would be decorated for Dia De Los Muertos. Below you can see the before/after.

Hint: Drag the blue circle left to right to compare the two images.

It looks much better right? But What is used to decorate it? Why? That is what we will get into. Now keep in mind that the following decoration steps are not in order. This is just the order that I happened to 3d model it in.

1. Candles. Candles are lit to guide spirits back to their graves, like a beacon. (also just to provide illumination)

Hint: Drag the blue circle left to right to compare the two images.

2. Marigolds. Marigolds are flowers that symbolize death, and they are believed to help guide spirits to their altars because of their smell.

Hint: Drag the blue circle left to right to compare the two images.

3. Ofrendas and Papel Picado are also used. Papel Picado is a traditional decoration for Dia De Los Muertos, and ofrendas are created as an alter for the spirits.

Hint: Drag the blue circle left to right to compare the two images.

And now you know how cemeteries are decorated and why.
